## Year 2017, Serie 1

### Problem 1
Consider the following system of linear equations, which depends on the parameter \( \lambda \):
\[
\begin{align*}
\lambda x + y - z &= 0 \\
y + z &= 10 \\
2\lambda x - y + 5\lambda z &= 30
\end{align*}
\]
1. Study for which values of the parameter \( \lambda \) the system is inconsistent.  
   [1 point]
2. Solve the system for the case \( \lambda = 1 \).  
   [1 point]

### Problem 2
Consider the planes \( \pi_{1}: 5x - y - 7z = 1 \) and \( \pi_{2}: 2x + 3y + z = 5 \).
1. Determine the general equation (i.e., the one in the form \( Ax + By + Cz = D \)) of the plane that passes through the origin and is perpendicular to the planes \( \pi_{1} \) and \( \pi_{2} \).  
   [1 point]
2. Calculate the angle formed by the planes \( \pi_{1} \) and \( \pi_{2} \).  
   [1 point]

### Problem 3
Let the function \( f(x) = \frac{1}{x^{2} - k} \), where \( k \) is a real parameter different from 0. For different values of the parameter \( k \):
1. Calculate the domain and asymptotes of the function.  
   [1 point]
2. Calculate the points with a relative maximum or minimum.  
   [1 point]

### Problem 4
We know that the following system of linear equations has a unique solution:
\[
\begin{align*}
x + ay &= 1 \\
x + az &= 1 \\
y + z &= a
\end{align*}
\]
1. Verify that \( a \neq 0 \).  
   [1 point]
2. Find the solution of the system in terms of the parameter \( a \).  
   [1 point]

### Problem 5
Consider the square matrices of order 2 of the form \( \boldsymbol{M} = \begin{pmatrix} x & -1 \\ y^{2} + 1 & x \end{pmatrix} \), with \( x \) and \( y \) real numbers.
1. Verify that the matrix \( \boldsymbol{M} \) is always invertible, regardless of the values of \( x \) and \( y \).  
   [1 point]
2. For \( x = 1 \) and \( y = -1 \), calculate \( \boldsymbol{M}^{-1} \).  
   [1 point]

### Problem 6

1. Verify that \( a^{2} = \frac{360}{\pi} \cdot \frac{1}{h} + h^{2} \).

## Year 2017, Serie 5

### Problem 1
Let the lines in \( \mathbb{R}^{3} \) \( r: \begin{cases} 2x - y = 1 \\ y - 2z = 0 \end{cases} \) and \( s: x + 1 = \frac{y - 2}{2} = z - 1 \).
1. Verify that they are parallel.  
   [1 point]
2. Calculate the vector equation of the plane that contains them.  
   [1 point]

### Problem 2
Consider the following system of linear equations:
\[
\begin{align*}
x - y + z &= 0 \\
2x + kz &= 1 \\
x + (k + 1)y + z &= k^{2} - 4
\end{align*}
\]
1. Discuss the system for different values of \( k \).  
   [1 point]
2. Solve the system for the case \( k = -2 \).  
   [1 point]

### Problem 3
1. Verify that the tangent line to the curve \( y = x^{2} \) at the point with abscissa \( x = 2 \) is the line \( y = 4x - 4 \) and calculate the intersection points of this line with the coordinate axes.  
   [1 point]
2. Calculate the area bounded by the curve from the previous part, the tangent line at \( x = 2 \), and the x-axis.  
   [1 point]

### Problem 4
Consider the matrix \( \boldsymbol{A} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -1 \\ -1 & 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \).
1. Calculate the powers \( \boldsymbol{A}^{2}, \boldsymbol{A}^{3}, \) and \( \boldsymbol{A}^{6} \).  
   [1 point]
2. Calculate the inverse of the matrix \( \boldsymbol{A}^{5} \).

### Problem 5

1. Discuss the system according to the values of the parameter \( a \), and interpret the result geometrically.  
   [1 point]
2. For \( a = 1 \) find the parametric form of the solution plane and give a point and two direction vectors of this plane.
