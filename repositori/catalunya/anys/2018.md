## Year 2018, Serie 1

### Problem 1
Let the matrices \( \boldsymbol{M} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 0 & -1 \\ t & 2 \end{pmatrix} \) and \( \boldsymbol{N} = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & t & 2 \\ 1 & 0 & -1 \end{pmatrix} \).
1. Calculate \( \boldsymbol{M} \cdot \boldsymbol{N} \) and verify that the resulting matrix is not invertible.  
   [1 point]
2. Find the values of \( t \) for which the matrix \( \boldsymbol{N} \cdot \boldsymbol{M} \) is invertible.  
   [1 point]

### Problem 2
Let \( r \) be the line passing through the points \( A = (0, 1, 1) \) and \( B = (1, 1, -1) \).
1. Find the parametric equation of the line \( r \).  
   [1 point]
2. Calculate all the points on the line \( r \) that are equidistant from the planes \( \pi_{1}: x + y = -2 \) and \( \pi_{2}: x - z = 1 \).  
   [1 point]

### Problem 3
Let the function \( f(x) = x^{3} - x^{2} \).
1. Find the equation of the tangent line to the graph that is parallel to the line with equation \( x + 3y = 0 \).  
   [1 point]
2. Calculate, if any, the points on the graph where the function has a relative maximum or minimum or an inflection point.  
   [1 point]

### Problem 4
Consider the points \( P = (3, -2, 1) \), \( Q = (5, 0, 3) \), \( R = (1, 2, 3) \), and the line \( r: \begin{cases} x + y + 1 = 0 \\ 2y + 3z - 5 = 0 \end{cases} \).
1. Determine the general equation (i.e., the one in the form \( Ax + By + Cz = D \)) of the plane that passes through \( P \) and \( Q \) and is parallel to the line \( r \).  
   [1 point]
2. Given the plane \( x + 2y + m \cdot z = 7 \) and the plane passing through \( P, Q, \) and \( R \), find \( m \) so that they are parallel and not coincident.  
   [1 point]

### Problem 5
Let the function \( f(x) = \sqrt{x} + x - 2 \).
1. Verify that the function \( f(x) \) satisfies the statement of Bolzano's theorem on the interval \( [0, 2] \) and that, therefore, the equation \( f(x) = 0 \) has a solution in the interval \( (0, 2) \). Verify that \( x = 1 \) is a solution of the equation \( f(x) = 0 \) and reason, considering the sign of \( f'(x) \), that the solution is unique.  
   [1 point]
2. Based on the final result of the previous part, find the area bounded by the graph of the function \( f(x) \), the x-axis, and the lines \( x = 0 \) and \( x = 1 \).  
   [1 point]

## Year 2018, Serie 5

### Problem 1
Consider the system of linear equations \( \begin{cases} 6x + 3y + 2z = 5 \\ 3x + 4y + 6z = 3, \text{ for } m \in \mathbb{R} . \\ x + 3y + 2z = m \end{cases} \).
1. Explain why for any value of the parameter \( m \) the system has a unique solution.  
   [1 point]
2. Solve the system and find the general expression of the solution point.  
   [1 point]

### Problem 2
Let the plane with equation \( \pi: x + y - z = 0 \) and the point \( P = (2, 3, 2) \).
1. Calculate the symmetric point of \( P \) with respect to the plane \( \pi \).  
   [1 point]
2. Calculate the Cartesian equation (i.e., the one in the form \( Ax + By + Cz = D \)) of the two planes parallel to \( \pi \) that are at a distance \( \sqrt{3} \) from the point \( P \).  
   [1 point]

### Problem 3
Let the function \( f(x) = a \cdot \mathrm{e}^{-x^{2} + bx} \), with \( a \neq 0 \) and \( b \neq 0 \).
1. Calculate the values of \( a \) and \( b \) that make the function have a relative extremum at the point \( (1, \mathrm{e}) \).  
   [1 point]
2. For the case \( a = 3 \) and \( b = 5 \), calculate the horizontal asymptote of the function \( f \) as \( x \) tends to \( +\infty \).  
   [1 point]

### Problem 4
We know that a function \( f(x) \) is defined for all real numbers and is twice differentiable. We also know that it has an inflection point at the point with abscissa \( x = 2 \), that the equation of the tangent line to the graph of the function \( f(x) \) at this point is \( y = -124x + 249 \), and that \( f(-3) = -4 \).
1. Calculate \( f''(2), f'(2) \), and \( f(2) \).  
   [1 point]
2. Calculate \( \int_{-3}^{2} f'(x) \, \mathrm{d}x \).  
   [1 point]

### Problem 5
Let the lines \( r_{1}: x - 1 = \frac{y - 2}{-1} = z - 5 \) and \( r_{2}: (x, y, z) = (2 - 3\lambda, -1 + \lambda, 2) \).
1. Find the Cartesian equation (i.e., the one in the form \( Ax + By + Cz = D \)) of the plane that contains the line \( r_{1} \) and is parallel to the line \( r_{2} \).  
   [1 point]
2. State the condition that must be met for there to exist a plane that contains the line \( r_{1} \) and is perpendicular to the line \( r_{2} \). With the lines \( r_{1} \) and \( r_{2} \) from the statement, verify if there exists a plane that contains the line \( r_{1} \) and is perpendicular to the line \( r_{2} \).  
   [1 point]
