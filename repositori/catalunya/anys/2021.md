## Year 2021, Serie 2

### Problem 1
Consider the parabola \( y = 4 - x^{2} \) and a value \( a > 0 \).
1. Verify that the equation of the tangent line to the graph of the parabola at the point with abscissa \( x = a \) is \( y = -2ax + a^{2} + 4 \) and calculate the intersection points of this tangent line with the coordinate axes.  
   [1.25 points]
2. Calculate the value of \( a > 0 \) so that the area of the triangle determined by this tangent line and the coordinate axes is minimal.  
   [1.25 points]

### Problem 2
Consider the following system of linear equations, which depends on the real parameter \( p \):
\[
\begin{align*}
p x + y + z &= 2 \\
2x + py + p^{2}z &= 1 \\
2x + y + z &= 2
\end{align*}
\]
1. Discuss the system for different values of the parameter \( p \).  
   [1.5 points]
2. Solve the system, if possible, for the case \( p = 2 \).  
   [1 point]

### Problem 3
Consider the point \( P = (-1, 3, 1) \), the plane \( \pi: x = y \), and the line \( r: \frac{x - 1}{2} = \frac{y}{3} = z - 2 \).
1. Find the coordinates of the point \( P' \) symmetric to \( P \) with respect to the plane \( \pi \).  
   [1.25 points]
2. Of all the planes containing the line \( r \), find the Cartesian equation of the one that is perpendicular to the plane \( \pi \).  
   [1.25 points]

### Problem 4
Let the function \( f(x) = \frac{\ln(x)}{x} \) be defined in the domain \( x > 0 \), where \( \ln \) is the natural logarithm.
1. Find the coordinates of a point on the curve \( y = f(x) \) where the tangent line to the curve is horizontal and analyze if the function has a relative extremum at this point.  
   [1 point]
2. Determine if the function \( f(x) \) has any horizontal asymptotes.  
   [0.5 points]
3. Calculate the area of the region bounded by the curve \( y = f(x) \) and the lines \( x = 1 \) and \( x = \mathrm{e} \). Make an approximate sketch of the graph of the function in the domain \( 0 < x < 5 \), representing the area you calculated.  
   [1 point]

### Problem 5
1. Given the matrix \( \boldsymbol{A} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \), solve the matrix equation \( \boldsymbol{A}^{2} \boldsymbol{X} = \boldsymbol{A} - 3 \boldsymbol{I} \), where \( \boldsymbol{I} \) is the identity matrix.  
   [1.25 points]
2. A square matrix \( \boldsymbol{M} \) satisfies \( \boldsymbol{M}^{3} - 3 \boldsymbol{M}^{2} + 3 \boldsymbol{M} - \boldsymbol{I} = 0 \), where \( \boldsymbol{I} \) is the identity matrix. Justify that \( \boldsymbol{M} \) is invertible and express the inverse of \( \boldsymbol{M} \) in terms of the matrices \( \boldsymbol{M} \) and \( \boldsymbol{I} \).  
   [1.25 points]

### Problem 6
Consider the function \( f(x) = \mathrm{e}^{x-1} - x - 1 \).
1. Study its continuity, relative extrema, and intervals of increase and decrease.  
   [1.25 points]
2. Prove that the equation \( f(x) = 0 \) has exactly two solutions between \( x = -1 \) and \( x = 3 \).  
   [1.25 points]

## Year 2021, Serie 5

### Problem 1
Consider the matrices \( \boldsymbol{A} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -3 & 4 \end{pmatrix} \), \( \boldsymbol{B} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \), and \( \boldsymbol{C} = \begin{pmatrix} 5 & 1 \\ 0 & 7 \end{pmatrix} \).
1. Argue that the matrix \( \boldsymbol{B} \) is invertible and then calculate \( \boldsymbol{B}^{-1} \).  
   [1.25 points]
2. Calculate the matrix \( \boldsymbol{X} \) that satisfies the equality \( \boldsymbol{A} + \boldsymbol{B} \cdot \boldsymbol{X} = \boldsymbol{C} \cdot \boldsymbol{A} \).  
   [1.25 points]

### Problem 2
Let the functions \( f(x) = x^{3} - 9x \) and \( g(x) = 7x \).
1. Study the intervals of increase and decrease of \( f(x) \).  
   [1.25 points]
2. Calculate the area of the region in the half-plane \( x \geq 0 \) bounded by the graphs of \( f(x) \) and \( g(x) \).  
   [1.25 points]

### Problem 3
Consider the points in three-dimensional space \( A = (1, a, 1) \), \( B = (a, 1, 2) \), \( C = (1, 1, 1) \), and \( D = (0, 0, 0) \), where \( a \) is a real parameter.
1. Determine the value of the parameter \( a \) for which the points are distinct and coplanar (i.e., there is a plane that contains them).  
   [1.25 points]
2. For the value \( a = 2 \), calculate the area of the triangle with vertices \( A, B, \) and \( C \).  
   [1.25 points]

### Problem 4
The breaking resistance \( R \) of a rectangular beam with base \( x \) and height \( y \) is directly proportional to the product \( xy^{2} \); thus, \( R = kxy^{2} \), where \( k \) is a positive constant. We have a wooden log in the shape of a cylinder with diameter \( d \) as shown in the figure.
1. Verify that the resistance \( R \) of the rectangular beam with base \( x \) that we can construct from this log is given by the expression \( R = kx(d^{2} - x^{2}) \).  
   [1.25 points]
2. Calculate the dimensions of the rectangular beam with maximum resistance that we can construct from this log and calculate this maximum resistance.  
   [1.25 points]

### Problem 5
Consider the following system of linear equations, which depends on the real parameter \( a \):
\[
\begin{align*}
x + 2y + az &= 8 \\
2x + y - az &= 1 \\
3x - 3az &= 1
\end{align*}
\]
1. Verify that, for any value of the parameter \( a \), the system of linear equations has no solution.  
   [1.25 points]
2. Interpret the system of linear equations geometrically. Make a schematic drawing representing the relative position of the three planes.  
   [1.25 points]

### Problem 6
Solve the following two questions:
1. Let \( f(x) = 2x^{3} + mx^{2} + nx + p \) be a function that has two relative extrema at \( x = -3 \) and \( x = 1 \) and passes through the point \( (3, 4) \). Calculate the values of \( m, n, \) and \( p \).  
   [1.25 points]
2. Calculate the equation of the tangent line to the function \( f(x) = \frac{1 - x}{1 + x} \) at \( x = -3 \).  
   [1.25 points]
